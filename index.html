Welcome to Yu-Gi-Oh!

If you are someone who was born in the mid to late 90's or early 2000's and watched a lot of Cartoon Network, chances are you may have seen a Japanese Anime by the name Yu-Gi-Oh!. When I was a kid I used to watch it whenever I could, facinated by the different art style, the monster designs, and the admittedly terrible plot. The monsters and other cards played though were by far the most interesting part of the show. Dark Magician and Red-Eyes Black Dragon were so cool, when I learned that there were real cards associated with the show I had to have them. My brother and I bought decks and would play against each other, just like in the anime.

But that was the problem, not only did the anime not follow the rules of the Trading Card Game (TCG), but my beloved Red-Eyes and Dark Magician were awful in the real game. I'd come back to the trading card game now and again even after I aged out of the anime. I always wanted to learn how to play but never had anyone else near me who was interested. And then COVID hit.

During quarantine I got really board and stumbled across a Yu-Gi-Oh! YouTube channel, Cimoooooooo, doing a series that went through all of the card sets ever released and dueling his friend. In watching them and others to follow I slowly learned how the TCG was actually played during the timeframe of the original Anime and I want to detail that here. Join me as I discuss the rules of the game vs how it was played in the original Anime. If you are completely new to the card game, I highly recommend starting with The Basics, but it is your choice! I will just be using game terminology throughout the journey and you may get a little lost if you arent familiar.
